Terms & Definitions (Jargon)

Wondering what people mean when they say they're looking for a RBHA, chained Pokémon? This page contains information about commonly used terms and phrases. Trading communities commonly use unique terminology and abbreviations when communicating with each other. This page will cover everything you need to know!

Legality

Term Definition
Legitimate A Pokemon that has been generated as intended by the game itself, with no cheat codes or third party edits.

Legitimate Pokemon are permitted for trading in the Wi-Fi room.
Legal A Pokemon that is possible to obtain by a player in game, regardless of how statistically unlikely it may be. A legal Pokemon is not always legitimate, but a legitimate Pokemon is always legal.

Legal, hacked Pokemon are banned from trading in the Wi-Fi room.
Illegal/Hacked A Pokemon that is impossible to achieve by a player in game, meaning cheat codes or third party edits were used to achieve a result.

Illegal/Hacked Pokemon are banned from trading in the Wi-Fi room.

Term Definition
Flawless IVs Refers to a Pokémon with ideal IVs for competitive battles, usually 4-6 Perfect 31 IVs. IVs of 0 are considered flawless if in the correct place, for the correct Pokémon, such as a Brave 0 speed Stakataka for Trick Room, or a special attacking Pokémon with 0 attack to take minimum damage from Foul Play.
Apricot Balls Specific Poké Ball types originally obtained from Apricorns in HGSS. They returned in later games, but became limited in quantities through natural gameplay. In Sword & Shield You can get more by beating the Champion's Cup or the Isle of Armor’s Cram-O-Matic. Examples include: Moon Ball, Fast Ball, and Friend Ball. Along with Apriballs, there are four more types of "Rare" Poké Balls: Dream Ball, Beast Ball, Sport Ball, and Safari Ball, which similarly reappeared later games, but became limited in quantity.
Battle Ready (BR) Refers to a Pokémon that is ready for competitive battle. BR Pokémon have the correct level, IVs, EVs, nature, and moveset for competitive battling.
Breedable Any Pokémon that can be bred to produce eggs.
Breedjects A shortened form of "Breeding Rejects." This refers to any breedable Pokémon left over from breeding. They usually have imperfections in IVs, nature, ability, etc.
Brilliant Aura In the overworld of Sword & Shield, some Pokémon have a yellow-golden aura surrounding them. This is a Brilliant Aura Pokémon. Catching them gives you Watts, and Brilliant Aura Pokémon have guaranteed 2 IVs alongside an Egg Move. You can increase your chance to find Brilliant Aura Pokémon by catching more Pokémon of that same species.
Chaining Chaining is a type of Wild Encounter method present in most recent games, which enables the player to hunt high IV Pokémon with increased shiny odds in wild battles! Chaining is commonly used in Gen 7 onwards.
Egg Moves (EMs) Moves that are passed onto offspring when a Pokémon is bred which cannot be learned through level-up, TRs, or TMs.
Effort Values (EVs) EVs increase the base stats of your Pokémon and are fundemental to building a strong competitive team. You can increase the EVs of your Pokémon by feeding them vitamins, or battling and defeating Pokémon of a specific species!
Effort Levels (ELs) ELs increase the base stats of your Pokémon in Legends: Arceus and are fundemental to making your Pokémon stronger! You can increase the ELs of your Pokémon by feeding them Grit, which you can obtain by releasing any unwanted Pokémon at the pasture!
Hidden Ability (HA) Refers to a Pokémon that has itss Hidden Ability.
Hidden Power "HP" is an abbreviation of "Hidden Power," which is a move whose type is dependent on IV spread. Hidden Powers are no longer obtainable in Gen 8.
Hyper Trained (HTed) In Gen 7 & 8, Hyper Training allows you to boost a Pokémon's IVs using bottle caps. A Hyper Trained IV behaves like it is 31 in battle, but breeding still uses the base IV. This allows Pokémon to have Hidden Powers without losing power by having imperfect IVs.
Imperfect Describes a Pokémon with any trait that makes it unsuitable for competitive use.
Individual Values (IVs) These are hidden numbers that range from 0 to 31 and affect a Pokémon's stats. Every stat has an IV and they are usually listed in the order of HP/Atk/Def/SpA/SpD/Spe. Usually, IVs only need to be stated if they are 0, 30, or 31; X can be used in place of unknown IVs.
Original Trainer (OT) The player name of the person who bred or caught the Pokémon which is found in the Pokémon's Summary page.
Secret ID (SID) A 4 or 5 digit identifier given to every player that appears in the data of any Pokémon caught or bred by them. This is a number that is used to calculate other values such as TSV and Generation 7 TID and is not visible through normal gameplay.
Shop Ball Refers to balls that can be purchased out of Poké Marts. Examples of these include the Luxury Ball, Great Ball, and Premier Ball.
Star & Square Shiny This refers to the sparkle animation your shiny Pokémon is given when sent out into battle in Sword & Shield. The effect is purely visual and the different shiny types have different rarities depending on the origin of your encounter.
Trainer ID (TID) Found on your Trainer Card in-game. In Generations 1-6, this is 5 digits, and in Generations 7 & 8, this is 6 digits.
Virtual Console (VC) Refers to Pokémon transferred from the Generation 1 and 2 games re-released on 3DS.
