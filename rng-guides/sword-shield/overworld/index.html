Sword/Shield Overworld RNG Guide

This guide will provide in-depth coverage of how to RNG abuse all types of overworld-based encounters in Sword/Shield using the SWSH Overworld RNG Generator by LegoFigure11. Overworld RNG abuse is best utilized to help players obtain shiny and/or marked Pokémon from wild encounters! This can also be used to calculate other internal values such as IVs, nature and gender.

This guide assumes you are using a CFW Switch console running Atmosphere.

Getting Started:

You will need:
  • SWSH Overworld RNG Generator - Version 2.1.0 or later
  • sys-usb-botbase
  • ldn-mitm
Installation Instructions:
  1. Download and extract all the contents from the SWSH_OWRNG_Generator_GUI.zip into an empty folder.
  2. Download and extract all the contents from the sys-usb-botbase and ldn-mitm downloads into an empty folder.
  3. Place the atmosphere folder(s) from these downloads onto the root of your Switch SD card and reboot the console.
You can check the atmosphere/contents folder of your SD card and make sure the 430000000000000B & 4200000000000010 folders are present before rebooting your console if you wish.

Monitoring the RNG state:

Once we connect our console to the tool we are able to monitor the RNG state in real-time. The number in the Curr. adv. field will display the amount of frames that have passed since we initially connected to the tool. The amount frames that advance naturally will heavily depend on your in-game environment and location. For the purposes of this guide we will be referring to slow-paced natural advancements as "Quiet" and fast-faced as "Noisy".

Environment Control:
The more noisy the environment the more difficult it becomes to control the speed of frame advancements, which in turn will increase the difficulty of actually hitting a desirable target frame.

There are three main contributors to the amount of natural noise in an area, these are:
  1. Location - Some areas will simply be more noisy than others.
  2. Weather - Extreme weather conditions such as rain, thunderstorm and sandstorm will have a large effect on noise.
  3. Other Pokémon that are present - Having other Pokémon spawned will advance the RNG state moreso than if there were none spawned.
Examples:
- In Crown Tundra the RNG is naturally quiet, so we will have a relatively easy time hunting a Pokémon in clear weather conditions.
- In Isle of Armor the RNG is naturally noisy, so we will have a more difficult time hunting Pokémon in general.
- If we added rainy weather to either of these situations we would increase the amount of natural noise, and further increase the instability.

In all cases it's best to target a Pokémon in a quiet area when possible for the sake of simplicity. (Some Pokémon can only be hunted in unstable overworld environments.)

Advancing the RNG state:

Multiple in-game actions can be used to advance the RNG state with varying influence, these are:
  1. Player movement - Each time you input a new direction on the control stick the RNG state will advance a small amount.
  2. Active NPCs - Standing near an NPC will "activate" them, this will advance the RNG state quite rapidly.
  3. Rotom Bike - Mounting and dismounting the bike will advance the RNG state a small amount, but moreso than player movement.
  4. Day skipping - We can use the day skip feature of the tool to advance the RNG state rapidly and will be our go-to mathod for large amounts of frame advancements.
  5. Menuing - Opening and closing the pause menu or map will advance the RNG state a small amount. This is primarily used for fishing encounters
  6. Flying - We can fly into an area to advance the RNG state too! This is used primarily for hunting Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres.
The amount of frames that advance by Day Skipping is heavily influenced by the amount of Pokémon currently in your boxes, the number will increase drastically if you store more Pokémon. (Up to 13,000 per day skipped with 32 full boxes for reference.)

The Process

This portion of the guide will cover the process of RNG abusing a Pokémon from each type of encounter. The types of encounter we will look at will be Static, Symbol, and Hidden encounters, we will also cover Galarian Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres on a seperate page. Each method we dive into will have a differing approach, so be sure to read through and grab an understanding of the type of encounter you are doing before making attempts.

First, lets give definitions to each type of encounter...
  • Static - These are the 100% chance-of-encounter spawns with an overworld model. Cannot be Brilliant Aura.
  • Symbol - These are the by-chance encounters in the grass with an overworld model. Can be Brilliant Aura.
  • Hidden - These are the by-chance encounters in the grass that do not have an overworld model, the "shaky grass" type of encounters. Cannot be Brilliant Aura.
Great! Now we know all the terminology, it's time to get started!

Decide on the type of encounter you want to do, to do this simply search the species you wish to hunt on the Encounter Lookup tab of the generator as seen above and pick one from the list.
