Raid RNG Guide (CFW)

This guide will provide in-depth coverage oF how to RNG abuse Max Raid Dens in Sword/Shield! Raid RNG abuse can be used to obtain shiny Pokemon and desirable IV/Nature combinations for any Pokemon in the dens.

This guide assumes you are using a CFW Switch console running Atmosphere and caters towards Windows devices.

Click here for the retail RNG abuse guide (unmodified Switch console)!

Download & Installation:

PC Applications:

Download Links:
  • RaidFinder
  • PKHeX
Installation Instructions:
  1. Download and extract all the contents from the RaidFinder download into an empty folder.
  2. Download and extract PKHeX.exe from the PKHeX download into an empty folder.
  3. Create a new folder inside the same directory as PKHeX.exe, called plugins.
Important Information:
  • If you are not using a device running Windows, you will need to find an alternative method of running .exe files in order to use PKHeX, running PKHeX on alternative operating systems will not be covered in this guide.
  • You will need to download a version of RaidFinder that is compatible with your operating system.
  • RaidFinder depends on Microsoft C++ Redistributable, download and install this before starting.

PKHeX Plugins:

Download Links:
  • PKHeX Plugins (For Livehex)
  • PKHeX Raid Plugin
Installation Instructions:
  1. Download and extract all the contents from the PKHeX-Plugins.zip into the plugins folder you made earlier.
  2. Download the PKHeX-Raid-Plugin.dll into the same plugins folder.
  3. Right click each of the plugins inside the folder and click Properties, click "Unblock" at the bottom of the Window that appears on screen. (If you do not see this button you will not need to follow this step.)
Important Information:
  • PKHeX plugins are used to significantly simplify the process of searching for raid seeds.
  • You can confirm your plugins are working by launching PKHeX.exe, if done correctly the Pokeball icon on the taskbar should now be blue.
  • You can find your plugins by clicking Tools on the main window of PKHeX. All of your installed plugins will be available here.
  • If you are experiencing issues when launching PKHeX.exe with the plugins, this is due to a plugins mismatch. You may need to download an older version of PKHeX that works with the latest version of the plugins you are using, or wait for the developers of the plugins to update them accordingly.

Environment Control:
For the purposes of this guide, the term "noisy" is used to describe an area or environment in which the RNG state naturally advances at a fast pace.

There are three main contributors to the amount of natural noise in an area, these are:
  1. Location - Some areas will simply be more noisy than others.
  2. Weather - Extreme weather conditions such as rain, thunderstorm, and sandstorm will have a large effect on noise.
  3. Other Pokémon that are present - Having other Pokémon spawned will advance the RNG state moreso than if there were none spawned.
Examples:
- In Crown Tundra, the area is naturally quiet, so we will have a relatively easy time hunting a Pokémon in clear weather conditions.
- In Isle of Armor, the area is naturally noisy, so we will have a more difficult time hunting Pokémon in general.
- If we added rainy weather to either of these situations, we would increase the amount of natural noise and further increase the instability.

In all cases, it's best to target a Pokémon in a quiet area. However, sometimes you will be forced into an unstable environment due to a lack of options.

Advancing the RNG State:

Multiple in-game actions can be used to advance the RNG state with varying influence, these include:
  1. Player movement - Each time the player stops moving the control stick, the RNG state will advance a small amount - Roughly 1-3 advancements per input.

  2. Rotom Bike - Mounting and dismounting the bike with the + button will advance the RNG state a small amount. - Area specific advancements.

  3. Menu close - Opening and closing the pause menu will advance the RNG state a small amount depending on your location and amount of NPCs in the area. - Area specific advancements.

  4. Animations - Having your Pokemon animate on the party summary screen will advance the RNG state a small amount. - One advancment per "click" on the party menu, 1 advancement per motion on the summary page.

  5. Active NPCs - Standing near an NPC will cause them to become active, this will advance the RNG state rapidly. - Area specific advancements, good for covering large amounts of advancements quickly.

  6. Map & Flying - Flying into an area will advance the RNG state too! - Area specific advancements, this is only used for Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres.

Day Skipping:

We can use DaySkip feature to advance up to a total of around 13,000 advancements per day skipped. The amount of frames that advance by Day Skipping is heavily influenced by the amount of Pokémon currently stored in your boxes (not including eggs). The number will increase drastically if you store more Pokémon.

If you plan to use the DaySkip feature, you should take a moment to monitor your advances to avoid overshooting your target frame. Connect to the GUI and skip a day at a time and take note of the amount of frames that have advanced.

The Process

There are four types of encounters: Static, Symbol, Hidden, & Fishing. Each method has differing techniques you can use, and it's important to understand the types of encounters before making attempts.

Galarian Articuno, Zapdos and Moltres are static encounters with a unique method of RNG abuse, for this reason they have been covered on a seperate page.

First, lets give definitions to each type of encounter...
  • Static - These are the 100% chance-of-encounter spawns with an overworld model. Cannot be Brilliant Aura.
  • Symbol - These are the by-chance encounters in the grass with an overworld model. Can be Brilliant Aura.
  • Hidden - These are the by-chance encounters in the grass that do not have an overworld model, they're indicated by the "!" symbol when walking into a patch of grass. Cannot be Brilliant Aura.
  • Fishing - These are the by-chance encounters in the water that do not have an overworld model, they're indicated by rippling areas in the water. Can be Brilliant Aura.
Great! Now that we know all the terminology, it's time to get started!

Search the species you wish to hunt on the Encounter Lookup tab of the generator and pick one of the encounters from the list. Take note of the type of encounter you have chosen and click on the page that applies to you!
